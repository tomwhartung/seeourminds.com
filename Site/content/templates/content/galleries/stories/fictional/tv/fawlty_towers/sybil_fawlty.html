In the Fawlty Towers hotel, Basil's wife Sybil Fawlty is undeniably the boss.

Sybil Fawlty: Red and Green, and Yellow

The Red and Green in Sybil's spiritual portrait shows she is decisive, and the Yellow — along with the almost total lack of any Blue whatsoever — shows she is interested only in facts.

And indeed she can be extremely closed-minded, especially when it comes to Basil's dreams of getting ahead in some way. There's never any hesitation in her voice, and it's like all of life's issues are already decided and everything is a done deal.

The Indomitable Sybil Fawlty

Actress Prunella Scales plays this role perfectly. Her formal manner and impeccable dress — down to the way she does her makeup and is always fussing with her hair and nails — contrasts strongly with Basil's farcical behavior, that tends to be increasingly manic as each episode progresses.

One of her funniest lines is a short and simple one, spoken frequently and repeatedly on the phone to her elusive friend Audrey:

Oh I know. I know!
 — Sybil Fawlty on Fawlty Towers, multiple episodes.

And indeed, this sums up her personality quite well, because if anyone in the Fawlty Towers hotel knows anything, it's Sybil Fawlty!

Sybil and the Team at Fawlty Towers

These spiritual portraits show that the management and staff at Fawlty Towers comprise a team of fairly diverse, interdependent individuals.

Sybil is Basil's wife, and her role is to support him in his position as manager. However, because her personality is so much more decisive than his — and most everyone else's — in reality her role turns out to be entirely dictatorial.

Sybil's need for nearly complete control over her husband — evidenced by the overwhelming amount of Red and Green in her image — is one of the main sources of humour in Fawlty Towers. The adjective nearly is essential here, because without at least a ray of hope, Basil would surely suffocate and die.

Sybil's most salient and distinctive quality on this team is her decisiveness, represented by the Red and Green in her spiritual portrait. Without her running things, albeit mostly through Basil, it's conceivable that nothing would get done — at least not properly.

Without Sybil, the entire team would be at risk of total failure, and without a new member — someone to provide the decisiveness that is lacking in the other members — to guide it, Fawlty Towers would surely go out of business, quickly and completely.
